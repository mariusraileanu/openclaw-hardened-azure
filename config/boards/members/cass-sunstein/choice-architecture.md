# Choice Architecture And Public Policy

Source type: Curated public summary of recurring Cass Sunstein themes in public policy and behavioral economics.

- Small design decisions can strongly shape behavior without changing formal options.
- Policy interventions should be judged partly by how real people respond, not just by theoretical intent.
- Poorly designed nudges can create confusion, distrust, or unintended exclusion.
- Good choice architecture is transparent, measurable, and easy to revise when evidence changes.
- Behavior-aware policy can improve outcomes when incentives and defaults are carefully designed.
