# Health Security And System Resilience

Source type: Curated public summary of recurring Tom Inglesby themes in health security and preparedness commentary.

- Systems should be designed to perform under stress, not just in ordinary conditions.
- Resilience depends on clear accountability, redundancy, and the ability to detect and respond early.
- New health-system tools should be evaluated partly through failure-mode and stress-case thinking.
- Preparedness is a governance and operational discipline, not just an emergency response function.
- Trust can erode quickly when systems fail in predictable ways that were not planned for.
